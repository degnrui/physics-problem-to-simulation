Problem Statement

A ball is thrown from the top of a 10-meter building with an initial velocity of 15 m/s at an angle of 30 degrees above the horizontal. Calculate the time of flight and the range of the projectile.

Key Concepts

  • Projectile motion
  • Horizontal and vertical components of velocity
  • Acceleration due to gravity
  • Time of flight calculation
  • Range calculation

Teaching Tips

Discussion Points:

  • How does changing the launch angle affect the range?
  • What happens to the time of flight if we double the initial velocity?
  • Why does the horizontal component of velocity remain constant?

Quiz Questions

2. If air resistance is neglected, which component of velocity remains constant?
